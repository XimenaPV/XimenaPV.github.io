TCP/IP Intro

Today's Vocabulary Definitions:

  • Protocol: Sets out basic rules on how computers send information to one another.
  • Packet: Tiny pieces of information.
  • Header: Where you are trying to the the information.
  • Payload: Information the computer is trying to get somehwere.
  • Router: Device that connects different packets to different networks.
  • Routing Table: Rules that a router has to use to determine the best path for sending a packet.

Problems that can occur on a packet-switched network:

  • Dropped packets, even though it usually happens to 1% of the total information.
  • Traffic is when routers get overwhelmed.
  • Wires get cut.
  • Hard to tell which way to send the packet.
  • Corruption.
  • Order.

Protocols we came up with in class to avoid the mentioned problems:

  • Packet sizes.
  • Include IP addresses when sending information.
  • Ask our neighboors who they are and who their neighboors are so we can figure out the web.
  • Use a BGP.
